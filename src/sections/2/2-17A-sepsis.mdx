<Note id="note-1" alignment="left">
  <ul>
    <strong style="display: block; text-align: center; margin-bottom:
    0.25rem;">
      IDENTIFICATION OF POSSIBLE SEPTIC SHOCK
    </strong>
    <li>Suspected infection – YES</li>
    <li>Evidence of sepsis criteria-YES (2 or more):</li>
    <ul>
      <li>Temperature less than 96.8 ° F or greater than 100.4 ° F</li>
      <li>Heart Rate greater than 90 bpm</li>
      <li>Respiratory rate greater than 22 bpm</li>
      <li>Systolic BP less than 90 mmHg OR Mean Arterial Blood Pressure (MAP) less than 65 mm Hg</li>
      <li>New onset altered mental status OR increasing mental status change with previously altered mental statu</li>
      <li>Serum Lactate level greater than 4 mmol/l-(if trained and equipment available)</li>
      <li>ETCO2 less than or equal to 25 mmHg </li>
    </ul>
  </ul>
</Note>

<EMTLevel level="basic">
  <ul>
    <MyLink to="/sections/1/1.0-routine-patient-care">
      <li>
        1.0 Routine Patient Care
      </li>
    </MyLink>
    <li>Notify hospital of incoming Sepsis Alert prior to arrival if applicable</li>
    <li>Supplemental oxygen to achieve SpO2 of 94%</li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="advanced">
<ul>
  <li>Full ALS Assessment and treatment</li>
  <li>Large bore IV access</li>
  <li>IV 0.9% NaCl enroute: administer 500 ml boluses up to 30cc/kg</li>

  Warning: assess lung sounds frequently to ensure volume overload does not occur
</ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="paramedic" medControl={true}>
  <li>
    Norepinephrine infusion by pump 0.1-0.5 mcg/kg/min IV/IO by pump, titrate
    to goal systolic Blood Pressure of 90mmHg, OR
  </li>
  <li>Epinephrine infusion 2-10 mcg/min IV/IO OR</li>
  <li>Dopamine 2-20 mcg/kg/min IV/IO.</li>
  <li>Additional Fluid boluses.</li>
</EMTLevel>
