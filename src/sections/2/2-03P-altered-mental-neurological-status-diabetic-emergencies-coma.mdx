<EMTLevel level="basic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      <MyLink to="/sections/1/1.0-routine-patient-care">
        1.0 Routine Patient Care
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      If patient is unconscious or seizing, transport on left side (recovery position).
    </li>
    <li>
      Glucose is indicated only for documented hypoglycemia. If authorized and
      trained to do so, obtain a blood sugar reading.
    </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        If glucose is known to be less than 70 mg/dL and the patient is conscious
        and can speak and swallow, administer oral glucose or other sugar source
        as tolerated.
      </li>
      <li>
        Oral glucose:
        <ul>
          <li>
            Â½ tube for a patient under 20 kg.
          </li>
          <li>
            1 tube for a patient 20kg or over.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </li>
    </ul>
    <ul>
      <li>
        Other sugar sources are acceptable.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <li>
      A second dose may be necessary after 10 minutes if patient remains
      symptomatic
    </li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="advanced">
<ul>
  <li>
    Treatment for specific etiologies, or coma of unknown etiology:
  </li>
<ul>

    <li>
      Known <strong>HYPO</strong>glycemia (glucose &lt; 70 mg/dL):
    </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        Dextrose 10% 0.5 gm/kg IV/IO.
      </li>
      <li>
        Glucagon 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO/IM up to max. of 1 mg.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <li>
      Known <strong>HYPER</strong>glycemia:
    </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        Administer 10mL/kg fluid bolus.
      </li>
    </ul>
  </ul>
</ul>
</EMTLevel>
