<EMTLevel level="basic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      <MyLink to="/sections/1/1.0-routine-patient-care">
        1.0 Routine Patient Care
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      If patient is unconscious or seizing, transport on left side (recovery position).
    </li>
    <li>
      Glucose or glucagon are indicated only for documented hypoglycemia. If
      authorized and trained to do so, obtain a blood sugar reading.
    </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        If glucose is known to be <strong>less than 70 mg/dL</strong> and the patient is conscious and
        can speak and swallow, administer oral glucose or other sugar source as
        tolerated.
      </li>
      <li>
        <strong>Oral glucose.</strong> One dose is one tube.
        <ul>
          <li>Other sugar sources are acceptable</li>
        </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
        A second dose may be necessary after 10 minutes if patient remains
        symptomatic.
      </li>
    </ul>
  </ul>
  <strong>
    IF the Patient is unconscious or unable to safely swallow and IF approved
    under
  </strong>
  <MyLink to="@TODO">
    Protocol 6.10 Glucagon for Hypoglycemia by EMT Basic:
  </MyLink>
  <ul>
    <li>Glucagon 1mg IM</li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        Recheck glucose 15 minutes after administration of glucagon.
      </li>
      <li>
        May repeat glucagon 1mg IM if glucose level is &lt;70mg/dL with
        continued altered mental status.
      </li>
    </ul>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="advanced">
  <ul>
   <li>
     For <strong>HYPO</strong>glycemic emergency:
   </li>
   <ul>
    <li>
      Dextrose 12.5 g IV/IO. Recheck glucose 5 minutes after administration of
      dextrose.
    </li>
    <li>
      May repeat dextrose up to 25 g IV/IO if glucose level is &lt;70mg/dL with
      continued altered mental status.
    </li>
    <li>
      Glucagon 1mg IV/IO/IM/IN if unable to establish IV access
    </li>
    <li>
      Recheck glucose 15 minutes after administration of glucagon.
    </li>
    <li>
      May repeat glucagon 1mg IV/IO/IM/IN if glucose level is &lt;70mg/dL
      with continued altered mental status.
    </li>
  </ul>
  <li>
    For <strong>HYPER</strong>glycemic emergency:
  </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        Administer 500mL fluid bolus, then 250ml/hr.
      </li>
    </ul>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>
