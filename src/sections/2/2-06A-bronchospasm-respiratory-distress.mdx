<EMTLevel level="basic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      <MyLink to="/sections/1/1.0-routine-patient-care">
        1.0 Routine Patient Care
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>IF</strong> the patient has not taken the prescribed maximum dose of their own inhaler prior to
      the arrival of EMS, <strong>AND</strong> the inhaler is present:
    </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        Encourage and/or assist patient to self-administer their own prescribed inhaler
        medication if indicated.
      </li>
      <li>
        If patient is unable to self-administer their prescribed inhaler, administer patient’s
        prescribed inhaler.
      </li>
    </ul>

    <strong style="display: inline-block; margin: 1rem 0;">
      NOTE: EMT-B and AEMT administration of an inhaler is CONTRAINDICATED, if:
    </strong>

    <ul>
      <li>
        The maximum dose has been administered prior to the arrival of the EMT.
        the patient cannot physically use the device properly. (Patient cannot receive inhalation
        properly.)
      </li>
      <li>
        The device has not specifically been prescribed for the patient.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <li>
      **If properly trained and authorized, use
      <MyLink to="/sections/6/6.01-bls-bronchodilators
">
        6.01 BLS Albuterol.
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      CPAP in accordance with:
      <MyLink to="/sections/6/6-09-continuous-positive-airway-pressure-emt-basic-and-emt-advanced">
        Protocol 6.9 Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP) by EMT-Basic and/or Advanced EMT.
      </MyLink>
    </li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="basic" medControl="{true}">
  <ul>
    <li>
      Additional doses of above medications, if prescribed to patient or authorized, and if
      maximum dose has not been administered.
    </li>
    <li>
      Epinephrine 0.3 mg via auto-injector. If approved under
      <MyLink to="/sections/6/6-06-check-inject-epinephrine-emt-basic">
        Protocol 6.6 Check and inject Epinephrine by EMT Basic
      </MyLink>
      epinephrine 0.3 mg IM.
    </li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="advanced">
  <ul>
    <li>
      Albuterol 2.5-3 mg via nebulizer. Ipratropium Bromide 0.5mg may be combined with the
      albuterol treatment.
      <br />
      Additional albuterol treatments may be administered as necessary
      with or without ipratropium bromide.
    </li>
    <li>
      Note that a multi-dose inhaler may be used to give albuterol or ipratropium (instead of
      nebulizer) if infection control is an issue (e.g. influenza-like-illness).
    </li>
    <li>
      If approved, epinephrine 1:1,000 0.3mg IM-ONLY.
    </li>
    <li>
      Must be administered in accordance with criteria listed in
      <MyLink to="/sections/appendices/A-01-adult-medication-reference">
        A1 Adult Medication Reference.
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      CPAP in accordance with
      <MyLink to="/sections/6/6-09-continuous-positive-airway-pressure-emt-basic-and-emt-advanced">
        Protocol 6.9 Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP) by
        EMT-Basic and/or Advanced EMT.
      </MyLink>
    </li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="paramedic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      In a patient with a known diagnosis of asthma or COPD, who does not have history
      or findings concerning for congestive heart failure, consider hydrocortisone 100 mg.
      IV/IO/IM or methylprednisolone 125 mg. IV/IO/IM.
    </li>
    <li>
      In patients ≤40 years old, epinephrine 0.3 mg IM as a one time dose.
    </li>
    <li>
      Continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) assistance, if not contraindicated, and if
    nebulizer therapy can be continued with the CPAP device.
    </li>
    <li>
      For Asthma only, consider magnesium sulfate 2-4 grams IV/IO over 20 minutes.
    </li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="paramedic" medControl={true}>
  <ul>
    <li>Additional doses of above medications.</li>
    <li>Epinephrine 1:10,000, 0.1-0. 5 mg IV/IO very slowly</li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<Caution id="caution-1">
  The use of Epinephrine in patients over the age of 40 or with known cardiac
  disease and patients who have already taken high dosage of inhalant bronchodilator
  medications may result in cardiac complications.
</Caution>

<Caution id="caution-2">
  Epinephrine for bronchospasm must be administered by Auto-Injector ONLY,
  except by medical control order or department authorization.
</Caution>
