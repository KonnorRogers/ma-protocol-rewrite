<EMTLevel level="basic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      <MyLink to="/sections/1/1.0-routine-patient-care">
        1.0 Routine Patient Care
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      Manage hypoglycemia and narcotic overdose per protocol.
    </li>
    <li>
      Consider eclampsia in a woman of childbearing age
    </li>

    <br />

    <strong> CAUTION: </strong> Do NOT administer anything orally if the
    patient does not have a reasonable level of consciousness and normal
    gag reflex.
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="advanced">
  <ul>
    <li>
      If Diazepam rectal gel (Diastat) has been prescribed by the patient’s
      physician, assist the caregiver with administration in accordance with
      physician’s instructions.
    </li>
    <li>
      If the patient has an implanted vagus nerve stimulator (VNS), suggest that
      the family use the VNS magnet to activate the VNS and assist if required.
    </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        To use the VNS magnet, pass the magnet closely over the VNS
        device; if unsuccessful, repeat every 3-5 minutes for a total of 3 times.
      </li>
      <br />
      Note: Do not delay medication administration.
    </ul>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="paramedic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      Cardiac Monitor and if feasible 12 lead ECG – Manage dysrhythmias per
      protocol.
    </li>
    <li>
      If patient is in Status Epilepticus
    </li>
    <ul>
      <li>
        Midazolam 2 - 6 mg slow IV/IO/IM
      </li>
      OR
      <li>
        Midazolam 10 mg IN
      </li>
      <li>
        Magnesium sulfate 2-4 grams IV/IO over 5 minutes if suspect eclampsia
      </li>
    </ul>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="paramedic" medControl={true}>
  Additional doses of above medications.
</EMTLevel>

<Caution id="caution-1">
  Benzodiazepines may be contraindicated in head injury or hypotension;
  discuss with medical control.
</Caution>

<Note id="note-1" alignment="left">
  <ul>
    <li>
      Post-partum patients may experience eclamptic seizures up to several
      weeks after giving birth.
    </li>
    <li>
      Status epilepticus is defined as any generalized seizures lasting
      more than 5 minutes.  This is a true emergency requiring rapid airway
      control, treatment (including benzodiazepines), and transport.
    </li>
  </ul>
</Note>
