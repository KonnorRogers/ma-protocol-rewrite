<EMTLevel level="basic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      <MyLink to="/sections/1.0-routine-patient-care">
        1.0 Routine Patient Care
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      Prevent patient from accidental self-harm. DO NOT use a bite block.
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>CAUTION: </strong> Do NOT administer anything orally if
      the patient does not have a reasonable level of consciousness and
      normal gag reflex.
    </li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="advanced">
  <ul>
    <li>
      If Diazepam rectal gel (Diastat) has been prescribed by the patient’s
      physician, assist the patient or caregiver with administration in accordance
      with physician’s instructions.
    </li>
    <li>
      If the patient has an implanted vagus nerve stimulator (VNS), suggest that
      the family use the VNS magnet to activate the VNS and assist if required.
    </li>
    <li>
      To use the VNS magnet, pass the magnet closely over the VNS
      device; if unsuccessful, repeat every 3-5 minutes for a total of 3 times.
    </li>
    <br />
    <strong>Note: </strong>do not delay medication administration.
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="paramedic">
  <ul>
    <li>
      If Glucose is less than 70mg/dL, treat per 
      <MyLink to="/sections/2/2.03P-altered-mental">
        2.3P Altered Mental/Neurological Status/Diabetic
        Emergencies/Coma- Pediatric.
      </MyLink>
    </li>
    <li>
      Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO/IM to a maximum single dose of 8 mg.
    </li>
    OR
    <li>
      Midazolam 0.2 mg/kg IN to a maximum dose of 10 mg.
    </li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<EMTLevel level="paramedic" medControl={true}>
  <ul>
    <li>Additional doses of above medications</li>
  </ul>
</EMTLevel>

<Caution id="caution-1">
  For patients under 12 years old, the airway is in most cases best
  managed with a BVM or SGA.  
  <br /><br />
  In some cases, intubation may be
  preferred. This is at the discretion of the treating paramedic.
</Caution>
