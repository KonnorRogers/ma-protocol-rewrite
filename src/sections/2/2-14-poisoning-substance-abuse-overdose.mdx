<Note id="note-1" alignment="left">
  Naloxone should only be administered in suspected overdose patients
  with inadequate respirations and respiratory rate. 

  <br /><br />

  Treatment should progress toward the restoration of adequate respirations. 
  
  <br /><br />

  Patients with inadequate respiratory rates may need to be ventilated until
  their respiratory rate increases.
</Note>

<br />

<EMTLevel level="first-responder" outside={true}>
  <EMTLevel level="basic">
    <ul>
      <li>
        <MyLink to="/sections/1/1.0-routine-patient-care">
          1.0 Routine Patient Care
        </MyLink>
      </li>
      <li>Naloxone 2 mg-4 mg via Nasal Atomizer (IN) or 0.4 mg via auto-injector (IM).</li>
      <li>If no response after 3-5 minutes, give second dose.</li>
      <li>First Responders may only administer if trained and authorized.</li>
      <li>If suspected or confirmed hypoglycemia, treat per protocol. </li>
    </ul>
  </EMTLevel>
